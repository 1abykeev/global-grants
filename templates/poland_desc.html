Direct Admission to Poland

Why choose Poland for education?
Poland is a country with high-quality education, affordable tuition and living costs, and plenty of opportunities for international students. Direct admission allows you to save time on complicated procedures and start studying at a European university as early as the upcoming semester.

Who can apply for education?
Any student with completed secondary education (high school diploma) can apply for a bachelor's program in Poland. It's important to prove proficiency in the language of instruction (Polish or English). This can be done through a language certificate such as IELTS, TOEFL, or certificates of B1/B2 level in Polish.

Application process:

  1. Choosing a university and program: Find a university and program that match your interests. Poland offers a wide range of fields such as engineering, business, medicine, and humanities.
  2. Filling out the application online: Most Polish universities accept applications through their online platforms or portals. You will need to register, select a program, and upload the required documents.
  3. Required documents:
    • High school diploma and its translation into English or Polish.
    • Language proficiency certificate (e.g., IELTS, TOEFL, or B1/B2 certificate).
    • Copy of your passport.
    • Motivation letter (not always required).
    • Resume (if required).
    • Passport-sized photo.
  4. Paying the registration fee: Universities charge a small registration fee (usually from 50 to 200 euros).
  5. Receiving an admission letter: After successfully submitting the application and document review, you will receive an admission letter, which is needed to apply for a student visa.

Language requirements:
If the program is conducted in English, proof of English proficiency is required (IELTS 5.5–6.5 or TOEFL). For studies in Polish, a B1 or B2 level certificate may be needed.

Application deadlines:

  • Summer semester: Apply by December or January.
  • Winter semester: Deadlines are usually by July or August.
Check the exact dates on the website of your chosen university.

Tuition fees and scholarships:
Tuition in Poland ranges from 2,000 to 5,000 euros per year, depending on the university and program. Some universities offer scholarships for international students that partially or fully cover tuition fees.

Obtaining a student visa:
After receiving the admission letter, apply for a student visa at the Polish consulate. You will need:

  • Admission letter.
  • Health insurance.
  • Financial guarantees (bank statement).
  • Completed visa application form and photo.

Important tips for applicants:

  • Start the process as early as possible to gather all the required documents.
  • Ensure you have translations of all documents into English or Polish.
  • Check the program requirements, as they may vary between universities.
  • Learn more about the city and university to prepare for life in Poland.

Direct admission to Poland is a real opportunity to receive quality European education with minimal complications!
